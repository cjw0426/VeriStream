\paragraph{Text-memory construction.}
All text-memory baselines retain the most recent four frames for the final answer and use sixteen
older frames as historical context. \textit{Uniform16} samples the historical prefix uniformly;
\textit{CLIP-topk16} selects the sixteen frames with the highest frozen-CLIP similarity to the
question. Selected historical frames are grouped four at a time. Qwen3-VL first produces one
English caption per group, and a text-only compression prompt rewrites the captions into at most
three short bullet points. The action-fact prompt emphasizes directly visible actions, object
locations, object states, and temporal events. The state-aware prompt emphasizes past state,
scene continuity, actor identity, and completed progress, explicitly avoiding the interpretation
that a past action is occurring in the current clip. The two variants are prompt-level,
training-free memory ablations rather than separately trained models.
They are introduced to disentangle two failure modes of long-video compression: losing concrete
events and confusing historical actions with the current state.

For example, for frames showing a person placing a red cup on a table and then leaving, the
action-fact prompt can produce ``A person places a red cup on the table and walks away.'', while
the state-aware prompt can produce ``The red cup is left on the table after the placement is
completed.'' The former preserves the event and action details; the latter emphasizes the
post-action state and reduces the risk of treating a past action as the current action.
